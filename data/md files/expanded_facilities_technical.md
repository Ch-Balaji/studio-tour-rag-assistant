- Refine animation (smooth, add details - 1 week)
- Facial animation and expressions (1 week)

**Phase 3: Effects (2 weeks)**
- Fire breath simulation (Houdini)
- Wing air displacement

**Phase 4: Lighting and Rendering (2 weeks)**
- Match lighting to live-action plate
- Test renders (low res to check look)
- Final renders (high res, 8 hours per frame)

**Phase 5: Compositing (1 week)**
- Combine dragon with live-action footage
- Color matching
- Add atmospheric effects (depth haze, etc.)
- Final touches and delivery

**Total Time**: 12 weeks for 12.5 seconds of finished dragon footage

**Process for Complex Makeups**:

**Example: Creating an Alien Character**

1. **Life Cast** (3-4 hours):
   - Cast actor's head and body parts needed
   - Creates perfect replica for prosthetic sculpting

2. **Sculpture** (40-80 hours):
   - Clay sculpture over life cast
   - Design alien features
   - Client approval on sculpture

3. **Mold Making** (20-30 hours):
   - Create multi-part mold
   - Must be precise for good fit

4. **Foam Running** (3-4 hours per piece):
   - Mix foam latex
   - Inject into mold
   - Bake in oven
   - Remove from mold

5. **Painting** (4-6 hours per piece):
   - Intricate paint job
   - Multiple layers for realism
   - Seal for durability

6. **Application on Actor** (2-4 hours daily):
   - Glue pieces to actor's face
   - Blend edges
   - Paint to match skin tones
   - Add final details

7. **On-Set Maintenance**:
   - Touch-ups between takes
   - Repair any damage
   - Keep actor comfortable

8. **Removal** (30-60 minutes):
   - Carefully remove pieces
   - Clean actor's skin
   - Store prosthetics if reusable
